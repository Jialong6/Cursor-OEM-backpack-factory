# Market Brief: German (de) — Germany / Austria / Switzerland

## Buyer persona

German-speaking brand owners, promotional-products agencies (Werbemittel),
outdoor/sport brands, and sourcing managers. The DACH B2B buyer is
detail-driven and risk-averse: compliance and verifiable claims outweigh
marketing flair. Industry reference points: HALFAR, europages.de, wlw.de.

## What DACH buyers care about (priority order)

1. **Zertifizierungen und Compliance** — ISO 9001, OEKO-TEX, GRS lead the
   argument. German buyers check certificates before prices.
2. **Qualitaet und Prozesssicherheit** — Japanese-brand track record reads
   as a strong proxy for process discipline; AQL inspection, X-ray needle
   control are concrete proof points to surface.
3. **Liefertreue** — fixed lead times beat fast-but-vague promises;
   "kapazitaetsgesicherte 4-Monats-Lieferzeit" is a selling point.
4. **Preis** — matters, but framed as Gesamtkostenvorteil (total cost), never
   "billig".
5. **Nachhaltigkeit** — recycled materials (RPET, GRS) increasingly a hard
   requirement for German brands.

## Language and register

- Durchgehend Sie-Form. Sachlich, praezise, keine Superlativ-Inflation und
  keine Ausrufezeichen.
- Industry vocabulary: Rucksackhersteller, Auftragsfertigung (OEM
  manufacturing), Musterfertigung (sampling), Mindestbestellmenge (MOQ) —
  first mention with abbreviation, then MOQ alone; Lieferzeit (lead time),
  Qualitaetskontrolle (QC), Eigenmarke / Private Label.
- Keep OEM, ODM, MOQ, QC, Tech Pack, AQL, FOB/CIF/EXW, YKK, Pantone in
  Latin abbreviations — standard on europages/wlw listings.
- Composite nouns natural: Produktionskapazitaet, Musterversand,
  Reissverschluss. Proper umlauts and eszett mandatory.
- Numbers: decimal comma, thousands dot (1.500 Stueck) — but keep source
  numeric values identical.

## Tone

Nuechtern-kompetent. Facts, processes, certificates. The German reader
should feel they are dealing with an auditable, process-mature supplier.
