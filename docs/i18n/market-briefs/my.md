# Market Brief: Burmese (my) — Myanmar

## Who reads the Burmese version

Unlike other locales, the Burmese page's primary readers are NOT foreign B2B
buyers. They are:

1. **Local talent and staff** — prospective employees, current staff families;
   the site doubles as an employer-brand page (600+ employees, stable factory).
2. **Local ecosystem** — Yangon suppliers, logistics partners, industrial-zone
   neighbors, banks, government/licensing contacts checking legitimacy.
3. **Burmese-speaking sourcing intermediaries** — local agents and QC staff who
   work for foreign buyers and verify factories on the ground.
4. **National-pride visitors** — a Myanmar factory serving Anello and Japanese
   brands is a point of pride; the Burmese page signals respect for the home
   country rather than an afterthought.

## What to emphasize (mindset rewrite)

- Stability and scale: since 2003, 600+ jobs, long-term Japanese partnerships —
  frame as "international standards, made in Myanmar by Myanmar hands".
- Local rootedness: Yangon location (Ngwe Pin Lai Industrial Zone), local
  employment, skills development.
- Trustworthiness: certifications, on-time delivery, transparent processes.
- Do NOT oversell "cheap labor cost" angles in Burmese — that framing reads
  as extractive to local readers; talk about competitiveness and craftsmanship.

## Language and register

- Formal written Burmese (standard literary register), polite endings
  (ပါသည် / နိုင်ပါသည်), no colloquial particles.
- Unicode Burmese only (never Zawgyi encoding).
- Keep OEM, ODM, MOQ, QC, Tech Pack, FOB/CIF/EXW, YKK, Pantone, ISO 9001,
  OEKO-TEX, GRS in Latin script — Myanmar garment/bag industry (CMP sector)
  uses these English terms verbatim; forced Burmese coinages read unnatural.
- Industry context: Myanmar's garment industry is known locally as the CMP
  (Cut-Make-Pack) sector — reviewers may use CMP as a reference frame.
- Numerals: Arabic digits (2003, 600+, 24, 10MB), not Burmese numerals.
- backpack = ကျောပိုးအိတ်; bag = အိတ်; factory = စက်ရုံ; sample = နမူနာ.
- Buttons/labels must stay SHORT: Burmese script runs ~30-50% longer than
  English; prefer compact phrasing for nav, buttons, and form labels.
  (Layout QA will check for overflow.)

## Tone

Respectful, proud, concrete. A serious Myanmar manufacturer speaking to its
own community in proper Burmese — not a translated brochure.
