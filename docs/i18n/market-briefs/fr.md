# Market Brief: French (fr) — France / Belgium (Wallonie) / francophone

## Buyer persona

French brand owners (fashion, maroquinerie-adjacent, outdoor), promotional
agencies, and sourcing managers. French buyers value design sensibility and
craftsmanship narrative alongside industrial reliability. Reference points:
europages.fr listings, fabricants de sacs à dos.

## What French buyers care about (priority order)

1. **Savoir-faire et qualité** — craftsmanship story (20+ ans d'expérience,
   partenariats japonais) resonates strongly; Japanese-brand collaboration
   signals excellence.
2. **Design et personnalisation** — full customization (couleurs Pantone,
   quincaillerie, tissus) presented as creative partnership, not just specs.
3. **Conformité** — OEKO-TEX, GRS, ISO 9001 matter for EU market access.
4. **Prix et MOQ** — competitive mais jamais "pas cher"; flexible MOQ helps
   young French brands.
5. **Communication** — reassurance about responsive, structured project
   communication (24 h).

## Language and register

- Vouvoiement throughout. Elegant but precise; avoid anglicisms where a
  natural French term exists.
- Industry vocabulary: fabricant de sacs à dos, production/fabrication OEM,
  devis (quotation), délai de production (lead time), échantillon (sample),
  échantillonnage (sampling), quincaillerie (hardware), tissu (fabric),
  marque privée (private label), guichet unique (one-stop),
  contrôle qualité (QC).
- Keep OEM, ODM, MOQ, QC, Tech Pack (with gloss "dossier technique" on first
  long-form mention), AQL, FOB/CIF/EXW, YKK, Pantone in Latin abbreviations.
- Typography: proper accents mandatory (é è ê à ç); use apostrophes
  typographiques where natural; espace before : ; ! ? per French convention
  ONLY in long-form copy (skip in short UI labels if layout-sensitive);
  guillemets « » for quotes in body copy.
- Numbers: keep source values identical.

## Tone

Professionnel et soigné — the copy of a confident maison industrielle:
precise about processes, warm about partnership.
