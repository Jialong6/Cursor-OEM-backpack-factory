# Market Brief: Korean (ko) — South Korea

## Buyer persona

Korean brand owners, sourcing MDs, and trading-company merchandisers looking
for a "China+1 / Vietnam+1" production base. Typical triggers: rising costs in
China/Vietnam, tariff diversification, K-fashion brands scaling bag lines.
They benchmark factories aggressively and expect fast, precise communication.

## What Korean buyers care about (priority order)

1. **Quality consistency** — Japanese-brand track record (Anello, 15+ years) is
   the single strongest trust signal for Korean buyers; lead with it. Japanese
   QC standards are understood as the gold standard in Korean sourcing.
2. **Communication speed and channel** — 24-hour response, and crucially the
   factory has a Japanese/Korean-speaking hotline (Ms. Cui) — surface this.
3. **Sample lead time and MOQ flexibility** — 2-week sampling, tiered MOQ.
4. **Price competitiveness** — Myanmar cost advantage vs China/Vietnam, but
   frame as value (quality at better cost), not "cheap".
5. **Compliance** — ISO 9001, OEKO-TEX, GRS matter for K-brands exporting to
   JP/US/EU.

## Language and register

- 합쇼체 throughout (-합니다/-입니다). Never 해요체 in body copy.
- B2B trade vocabulary: 견적 (quote), 발주 (order placement), 납기 (delivery),
  검품 (inspection), 봉제 (sewing), 원단 (fabric), 부자재 (hardware/trims),
  사양 (specifications), 양산 (mass production), 선적 (shipment).
- Loanwords in standard 외래어 표기법: 백팩, 샘플, 지퍼, 테크팩, 커스터마이징.
- Keep OEM, ODM, MOQ, QC, FOB/CIF/EXW, YKK, Pantone, ISO 9001, OEKO-TEX, GRS
  in Latin script — standard in Korean B2B sourcing.
- backpack = 백팩 (industry/fashion standard; 배낭 reads outdoorsy/military
  and dated for OEM fashion context).
- Titles and CTAs: concise noun-ending style is natural for buttons
  (예: "견적 요청", "문의하기"); full sentences for body copy.

## Tone

Professional, precise, quietly confident. Korean B2B copy trusts specifics
(numbers, certifications, process steps) over adjectives. Avoid exclamation
marks and hard-sell phrasing.
