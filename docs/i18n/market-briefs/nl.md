# Market Brief: Dutch (nl) — Netherlands / Flanders

## Buyer persona

Dutch and Flemish brand owners, promotional-products agencies
(relatiegeschenken), and sourcing managers. Dutch B2B buyers are pragmatic,
price-aware, and direct; they read English fluently but prefer Dutch for
trust. Reference frame: europages.nl, wlw-style supplier platforms.

## What Dutch buyers care about (priority order)

1. **Prijs-kwaliteitverhouding** — the Dutch buying lens; Myanmar cost
   advantage lands well when paired with proof of quality discipline.
2. **Betrouwbare levertijd** — fixed 4-month capacity-locked lead time as
   a concrete promise.
3. **Certificeringen** — ISO 9001, OEKO-TEX, GRS; sustainability
   (duurzaamheid) is a mainstream requirement in NL.
4. **Duidelijke communicatie** — direct, no-nonsense; 24-uurs reactietijd.
5. **Maatwerk en MOQ-flexibiliteit** — for scale-ups and agencies.

## Language and register

- U-form for B2B copy (u/uw). Direct and concise — Dutch copy tolerates and
  expects brevity; trim marketing fluff harder than in other languages.
- Industry vocabulary: rugzakfabrikant/rugzakkenfabriek, OEM-productie,
  offerte (quote) — "offerte aanvragen" is THE standard CTA, levertijd
  (lead time), monster/sample (sample; "sample" is common in NL trade),
  maatwerk (customization), stof/materiaal, fournituren (hardware),
  kwaliteitscontrole (QC), private label (English term is standard in NL),
  duurzaamheid (sustainability), totaaloplossing (one-stop).
- English loanwords acceptable where common in Dutch trade (sample, tech
  pack, private label) — Dutch B2B reads hybrid naturally.
- Keep OEM, ODM, MOQ, QC, Tech Pack, AQL, FOB/CIF/EXW, YKK, Pantone in
  Latin abbreviations.
- Numbers keep source values identical.

## Tone

Nuchter en direct — pragmatic, concrete, zero hype. The Dutch reader should
get facts fast and feel no sales pressure.
