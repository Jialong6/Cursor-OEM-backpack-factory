# Terminology Sheet (authoritative for all translations)

This sheet is injected into every translation/review prompt. Rules here override
model defaults. When a term is not listed, follow the target market's B2B
sourcing convention; when in doubt, keep the English term.

## 1. Never translate (all languages)

- Brand and proper names: Better Bags, Better Bags Myanmar, Anello, I-pack,
  Carrot Company, Yangon, Ngwe Pin Lai Industrial Zone, Myanmar plot addresses
- Certifications and standards: ISO 9001, OEKO-TEX (Standard 100), GRS, GOTS,
  AQL (e.g. AQL 2.5), Pantone / PMS
- Industry abbreviations (universal B2B sourcing lingua franca):
  OEM, ODM, MOQ, QC, BOM, FOB, CIF, EXW, PP Sample, TOP Sample, TPU, PU, PVC
- Materials / trade names: YKK, Cordura, ripstop, 600D / 900D / 1000D (denier
  values), RPET
- File/tech formats: Tech Pack stays "Tech Pack" unless the per-language row
  below says otherwise (a local gloss in parentheses on first occurrence is
  welcome in long-form copy)
- Contact identifiers: emails, URLs, phone numbers, WhatsApp

## 3. Per-language conventions

- **de**: Sie-Form durchgehend. Composite nouns natural (Rucksackfertigung).
  Decimal comma. Em-dash sparingly; prefer Klammern oder Kommas.
- **fr**: vouvoiement. Espaces insecables before : ; ! ? per French typography
  (use regular space if layout breaks). Proper accents mandatory.
- **es**: neutral Latin-American-leaning Spanish (ustedes, avoid vosotros;
  avoid region-specific slang). "mochila" not "morral".
- **pt**: Brazil-leaning neutral (voce/empresa), acceptable in Portugal.
  "cotação" for quote.
- **nl**: u-form for B2B copy. English loanwords acceptable where common
  (sample, tech pack).
- **ru**: formal Вы. OEM/ODM/MOQ stay Latin. Currency/number formats with
  non-breaking thin spaces are fine as regular spaces.
- **ko**: 합쇼체(-합니다/-입니다) throughout. B2B trade vocabulary (납기, 검품,
  발주, 사양). Loanwords in 외래어 표기법 spelling (백팩, 샘플, 지퍼).
- **my**: formal written Burmese (စာပေအရေးအသား), polite sentence endings
  (ပါသည်/နိုင်ပါသည်). Unicode only (never Zawgyi). Keep OEM/ODM/MOQ/QC and
  other Latin abbreviations in Latin script. Use Arabic numerals (2003, 600+)
  not Burmese numerals, matching modern Burmese business writing.
- **zh-tw**: Taiwan conventions — 品質(not 质量), 後背包, 客製化, 物流,
  資訊, 軟體/硬體 irrelevant here but follow TW norms; full-width punctuation.

## 4. Numbers, claims, facts

Never alter: years (since 2003, 20+ years), staff count (600+), response time
(24 hours), sampling time (2 weeks), MOQ figures, file limits (5 files, 10MB),
addresses, phone numbers. Formatting may follow locale conventions, values may not.
